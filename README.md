# Buzzword-1

**What is it?**
-
The first rocket I have ever made. It is a model rocket capable of reaching a max altitude of 400+ meters. It uses a G112 EX motor using KNSB and a CO2 ejection system. 

**How it roughly works**
-
This rocket comes in 3 main parts, the rocket motor, the body, and the recovery system. The rocket motor is custom made using aluminum and steel. To load it, you must cast KNSB grains, make a liner and make spacers before loading all of it into the case. You can use any igniter to ignite the propellant. The body consists of 3D printed segments glued together using epoxy. The recovery system uses a CO2 piercer. Due to where I live, blackpowder (which is traditionally used as an ejection charged) is outlawed forcing me to use alternatives. You first need to push the spring back and lock it in place using a pin and servo. You can then load a 12g CO2 cartridge into the mechanism. It's a lot more detailed than this and you can watch the YouTube video I plan to make about this if you want more info.

**Why I made it** 
-
Ever since I was 5 years old, I have always been very interested in aviation. I began learning about different aircraft at the age of 7, and it soon became a hobby of mine to study various planes. This eventually led me to playing Kerbal Space Program, where I designed different types of jet-powered aircraft. After about a month of playing (when I was around 13 years old), I tried to build my first rocket to orbit Kerbin (KSP's version of Earth). That was when I began to get fascinated with rockets. However, it wasn't until I was 15 that I started seriously learning about rocket mechanics, the history of rocketry, and large-scale rockets.
I wanted so badly to build my very own rocket, but the cost of manufacturing even a model rocket held me back. It was only when I discovered the Blueprint program that I immediately got to work.
